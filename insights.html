Fundamental Questions

Core inquiries that guide our research and shape our understanding of what makes enterprises endure across generations.

SYSTEMS & ADAPTATION

What patterns allow some businesses to improve with age while most degrade over time?

Our research into century-old companies reveals specific organizational patterns: they prioritize maintenance over innovation, build redundancy into critical systems, and cultivate adaptive capacity.

Explore this question

QUALITY & CRAFT

Why do we struggle to perceive quality in its early stages, yet recognize it instantly in mature form?

Quality emerges from complex system properties that individual parts don't exhibit. Like identifying a future oak in an acorn, recognizing nascent quality requires understanding patterns of potential.

Explore this question

TIME & MAINTENANCE

How does the relationship between maintenance and growth shift as systems mature?

Geoffrey West's research shows that organisms dedicate increasing energy to maintenance as they mature. We see parallel patterns in enduring businesses that shift from growth-obsession to excellence.

Explore this question

VALUE CREATION

What makes trust networks compound in value while transactional relationships decay?

Peter Kaufman identifies "deserved trust" as creating compound returns through reduced transaction costs, expanded opportunity access, and resilience during disruptions.

Explore this question

LEADERSHIP & CULTURE

Why do founder-led businesses dramatically outperform professionally managed ones over decades?

Todd Combs notes that founders operate as fiduciaries with radically longer time horizons. They make decisions that seem irrational quarterly but compound advantageously over generations.

Explore this question

RESILIENCE & RISK

How do enduring enterprises balance redundancy with efficiency in their systems?

Century-old companies build strategic redundancy into critical systems while maintaining operational efficiency—a paradox that creates both resilience and competitive advantage.
